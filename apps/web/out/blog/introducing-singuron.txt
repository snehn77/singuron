5:T5ae,<h2 id="the-research-behind-it">The research behind it</h2>
<p>Singuron's telemetry is not an afterthought — it is built on a peer-reviewed research framework called <strong>Selective Rigidity</strong>, published in 2026 at the ICML FinAgent Workshop, the COLT LAW track, and IEEE AICSIP.</p>
<p>The problem it addresses: agents operating over long horizons are vulnerable to adversarial social pressure — gaslighting, authority override, manufactured consensus, and memory poisoning. Our impossibility result (Theorem 1) proves that any safeguard observing only an agent's current output cannot exceed a Selective Rigidity Score of ¼ + ε. You need a signal on the agent's identity <em>trajectory over time</em> to do better.</p>
<p>Our answer is <strong>ICC + TSM</strong>. The Identity Continuity Constraint is a three-layer gate that blocks identity-violating updates while accepting legitimate corrections. The Temporal Self-Model tracks how identity evolves over time; its prediction-error spikes and cumulative-drift signals are what let ICC break the ¼ ceiling. Across 240 runs, ICC+TSM reached SRS = 0.260 (95% CI 0.212–0.306) — the only method to exceed the stateless ceiling while keeping AcceptRate above 0.75. We evaluate on the <strong>Identity Erosion Chamber</strong>, a 70-turn adversarial benchmark released open-source under MIT / CC-BY-4.0.</p>
<p>Every Singuron agent is the first fleet validated under this framework.</p>
